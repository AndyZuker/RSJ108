Setting Up a Tripod Correctly ...

Assembly time: 2 minutes
Awesome rating: 50% awesome

Setting up a tripod properly is a crucial part of filming a news segment on location. Follow these steps to set up the perfect shot!

  • Things You'll Need

  • Tripod
  • Camera

  1. Decide on your location.

    Before you start setting up your tripod, it is best to know where you want to film. Be sure the backdrop will work for your segment, and decide on the direction you will want to shoot.

  2. Arrange the legs.

    Point one of the legs in the same direction that your camera will face. Doing so will create a space between the other two legs for you to stand. This will make it easier and more comfortable for you to work while focusing and shooting.

    Tripod and Chill

  3. Extend the legs.

    Open the latches that release the legs and extend the sections. Start by extending the thickest sections first as they will provide more stable support than the thinner segments.

  4. Be sure the legs are secure.

    If you are on an uneven surface you will have to readjust the legs until you can get the length just right. When on soft ground or sand, push the tripod legs all the way down until it is pressed against something solid.

  5. Check the level on the tripod.

    Some models come with a level built in on the top of the tripod. Otherwise, you can step back and make sure the center post of the tripod is perpendicular to the ground.

    Gotta Get On The Level

  6. Avoid raising the center post.

    If you set up your tripod and realize you need your camera to be higher, adjust the legs first. The three legs of the tripod provide much better stability. Using the center post will make your setup less stable. Only use the center post as a last resort, or to make minor adjustments when you need an inch or two of additional height.
